Infectious Diseases

This is one in which animals are invalided by a foreign organism. Such as virus, bacterium or parasite.

Infectious disease require intermediate agent in other to spread from one animal to another. Examples are the equine infectious, anemia of horse can only be spread from one animal to another by blood sucking-biting flies.

Contagious Diseases

This is one that does not require an intermediate to spread from animal to another. Example is the contagious bovine pleuropneumonia (CBPP). Animals are infected by inhalation of infected droplets discharge by nearby clinical case.

Another example is the spore of the fungi that cause ringworm (a skin disease). These spores can survive in environment and can be source of infection to susceptible animals.

Understanding the routes of transmission of disease will help in the knowledge of how to prevent infections.

Venereal and Contagious Infectious Diseases

Venereal infectious are transmitted by coitus (mating). Disease can be transmitted from an infected male to a susceptible female or vice versa. Congenital infectious are transmitted from infected to offspring during pregnancy. Example include Hog cholera In pregnant sow the virus that cause hog cholera can cross through the placenta and infect the offspring which can then be aborted or born alive but deformed.

This route of transmission is sometimes called Vertical Transmission

Arthropods

There are of different species that include flies, lice, ticks, mite and bugs. Many are parasite of the skin of domestic animals and can cause serious skin irritations and damage. The resulting invasions of tissues by these arthropods are called MYIASIS and can cause great stress to the animals under attack.

Some arthropods are HEAMATOPHAGOUS i.e. they have piercing mouth-part which they use to take blood meals. Some arthropods can be seen with the naked eyes while some mites (small arthropods) that burrows into the skin and cause a disease called MANGE requires the aid of microscope.

Arthropods-borne Diseases

This type of disease is spread from animal to animal by biting arthropods. Biting arthropods taking blood meal from infected animals carries the disease (thereby act as a vector) and when taking blood meal during it next meal on a susceptible animal it infects the animals.

The infected agent undergoes multiplication in the vector which may remain infected for long periods.

Transmission can also be mechanical when blood sucking insect transfer infected blood directly from one animal to another.

Helininth Infection (Worms)

Helminthes (round-worms, thorny-headed worms and flat worms) animals are major cause of diseases and loss of productivity throughout the tropics.

Diseases associated with environment and Husbandry Factors

Some diseases arise as a consequence of their environment or the way animal are managed by the farmers. These include certain infectious diseases, metabolic disturbance, nutritional deficiency, poison and a variety of miscellaneous conditions. Certain infectious disease are triggered by environmental or husbandry factors. These factors are called PREDISPOSSING FACTORS.

Examples of infectious disease include

  • Mastitis in modern dairy herds
  • Clostridia toxemic
  • Metabolic disturbance like milk fever
  • Nutritional deficiency and imbalances
