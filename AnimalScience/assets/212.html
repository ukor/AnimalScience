ZOOLOGICAL CRITERIA FOR LIVESTOCK SPECIES

Species are all animals capable of mating/ breeding successfully for reproductive discontinuity for example the horse and the ass can actually mate and produce an offspring that is known as the mule which itself is not fertile, also the cattle and buffalo can produce a hybrid called cattalo which also is infertile. These hybrids form a border line between these species. There is a great variety of individual in one specie, so we need other criteria of classification, in order to decide where an individual animal actually being spp can therefore subdivided into four different distinguishing criteria

  1. Geographical criterion
  2. Ecological Criterion
  3. Economic Criterion
  4. Physiologic Criterion

Geographical Criterion

Geographical classification is based on the geographical area where an animal originates, this used to dominate the literature in animal science e.g. WAD goat, Albani cattle, Jersey in USA.

Ecological Criterion

These classifies the animal based on the environment which the animal is adapted to, it is necessary to know the climate to which the animal is adapted, it is important in particular if that animal is imported for crossbreed and for specific product. Most of the animals imported especially from the temperate region to the tropics proofed to be total failure because the animal never adapted to the new environment.

Economic Criterion

These criterion is very important for animal classification. Some animals are utilize for more than one product. We attach importance to the products depending on the cost of production, price of products and demand and supply some animals serve one purpose, other serve two purposes (dual purpose), while others serve more than two purposes (multi purpose).

Physiologic Criterion

This is closely connected with the performance of the animal, since the physiology of the animal influences the type, quantity and quality of product provided to man.

Animal ecology is the study of the relationship between animal and its environment wild animals are said to be in complete equilibrium of balance with the environment, and this environment consist of some physical characteristics of the atmosphere, vegetation, competition among the animals themselves and disease.

During the process of domestication, man relocates the animals there environment in various degrees, while beef cattle are subjected totally to the environment in ranches. Other specie like the domestic fowl is protected completely in battery cages from the natural environment. Ruminants are allowed to graze outdoors; pigs are kept completely indoors except for some of our local pigs reared by some peasant farmers.

Metabolic heat production depends on

  1. Basal heat production to maintain essential body temperatures such as core temperature, cardiovascular respiratory activities and muscle tone.
  2. Digestive heat production which varies with the type of digestive system, the quantity and quality of food ingested
  3. Muscular heat production that varies according to how much the animals move about grazing playing, fighting, mating etc
  4. Increased metabolism due to productive processes such as growth milk or egg production and reproduction

Generally, the means by which animals can vary their heat production are limited when compared with the means by which they can dissipate heat.

Animal Response to Heat Loss

When an animal is subjected to high ambient temperatures. It attempts some compensatory measures in these order, increased skin blood flow through vasodilatation of the blood vessels near the surface initiation or sweating: increased through shallow respiration (panting) changes in the hormones activity, increased use of water etc.
